# Grievance, Correction, Right-of-Response and Takedown Policy (TEMPLATE — requires counsel review)

**Grievance Officer (the operator jurisdiction-based):** [name] · [email] · [postal address]
Acknowledgement target: **24 hours**. Resolution target: **15 days**
(aligned with IT Rules 2021 grievance expectations).

## Who can complain
Anyone — especially any person or organization referenced in published
material, any source, any data principal exercising DPDP rights.

## Channels
Email [email] with subject "GRIEVANCE"; or the /grievance form (when enabled).
Each complaint receives a tracking ID and enters the immutable audit history.

## What we commit to
1. **Correction:** If published material contains an error, we correct it.
   Every correction triggers fresh two-person review before republication and
   is visibly logged in the item's correction history.
2. **Right of response:** A referenced party's response is published alongside
   the item ("authority responses beside allegations" is a core product rule).
3. **Withdrawal/takedown:** Where material should not remain public (legal
   order, safety risk, unsupported claim), we withdraw it and publish a
   tombstone noting withdrawal — we do not silently delete.
4. **Escalation:** Unresolved complaints may be escalated to [named incident
   owner]; DPDP complaints may go to the Data Protection Board of the operator jurisdiction.

## What we will not do
We will not keep publishing a claim that fails review. We will not identify
protected persons. We will not fabricate "verified" status — "not found" means
"not located in searched sources", never "does not exist".

## Security incidents
Security contact: [named CERT-In point of contact, email].
Covered incidents are reported to CERT-In within the required window (as fast
as 6 hours for specified incident types) and affected users are notified per
DPDP breach-notification obligations.
